# MedEdge Go-to-Market Plan

## Market Overview

The US dermatology services market is large, fragmented, and underserved by technology.

- Approximately 9,000-11,000 dermatology practices in the US
- About 14,000+ practicing dermatologists
- 40% are solo practices, 73% have fewer than 5 physicians
- The top 4 industry players account for only 2% of total market share
- 14-20% claim denial rates in derm (3x the industry norm)
- Manual prior authorizations take 7-14 days on average
- PE-backed consolidation is accelerating, creating demand for operational tools

Our addressable market is the solo and small practice segment: roughly 6,500-8,000 practices with 1-5 providers. These are the practices too small for enterprise PA tools (Cohere Health, Humata Health) but too busy to manage PA manually. They're the ones turning away patients from PA-heavy payers because nobody has time to deal with it.

At our average price point ($299/provider/month), a 3-provider practice pays $10,764/year. Capturing just 1% of the addressable market (65-80 practices) would generate $700K-860K in ARR.

## Go-to-Market Phases

### Phase 1: Design Partner (Months 1-6)

**Goal**: Build the product with Toby, prove it works, get the first case study.

**Activities**:
- Toby (Plaza Park Dermatology) is our design partner and first pilot customer
- Weekly feedback loops during pilot: what works, what doesn't, what's missing
- Document everything: time saved, PAs processed, approval rates, revenue impact
- Build the first case study from real data: "Plaza Park Dermatology processed X PAs in Y weeks, reduced turnaround from Z days to W days, and recovered $N through auto-appeals"

**Investment**: $0. Toby is already committed. The product is the investment.

**Key metric**: Does Toby keep using it every day after the pilot? If yes, proceed to Phase 2.

### Phase 2: Warm Network (Months 6-12)

**Goal**: Get to 10 paying practices through your dad's network. Zero marketing spend.

**Channel: Dad's Dermatology Lab Sales Network**

Your dad is a dermatology lab sales rep in NY with established relationships at dozens of practices. This is the unfair advantage. He's already in the building, already trusted, and already talking to the people who make buying decisions.

**The pitch (for dad to deliver during his normal rounds)**:
"My son built something for practices like yours. It handles prior authorization so your staff doesn't have to spend all day on the phone with payers. Plaza Park Dermatology has been using it and [insert specific result from case study]. Can I have him send over a quick revenue report showing how much your practice could be earning from payers you're not accepting?"

**The Revenue Radar hook**:
Before the product is even mentioned, generate a Revenue Radar report for the prospect's ZIP code. Show them: "There are X patients with [payer] insurance within 10 miles of your office. You don't accept [payer]. Here's what that's costing you." This is the door opener. It makes the conversation about growth, not software.

**Onboarding flow for warm leads**:
1. Dad mentions MedEdge during a lab visit
2. Practice owner or billing manager expresses interest
3. You send a personalized Revenue Radar report (5 minutes to generate)
4. You schedule a 20-minute video call to walk through the report and demo MedEdge
5. If they want to proceed, they sign up and you onboard them personally
6. First month is free (or heavily discounted) to reduce friction

**Target geography**: NY metro area first (where dad's network is strongest). Long Island, Queens, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Westchester, northern NJ.

**Target practices**: Small derm practices (1-5 providers) using ModMed PM. Don't waste time on non-ModMed practices until the second integration is built.

**Key metric**: 10 paying practices by month 12. At $299/provider average with 3 providers each, that's ~$8,970/month MRR ($107K ARR).

### Phase 3: Expand Distribution (Months 12-18)

**Goal**: Get to 30-50 practices. Start building repeatable sales motions beyond dad's network.

**Channel 1: Referrals from existing customers**
The best leads in healthcare come from doctor-to-doctor referrals. Dermatologists talk to each other at conferences, local medical societies, and professional groups. Build a simple referral program:
- Existing customer refers a new practice that signs up
- Referring practice gets one month free
- No complicated tracking. Just ask "who referred you?" at signup.

**Channel 2: ModMed Marketplace listing**
Once you have 10+ practices and a track record, apply to be listed on the ModMed Marketplace. This puts MedEdge in front of every ModMed user when they browse integrations. ModMed may require a partnership agreement and technical review.

**Channel 3: Content and thought leadership**
Publish the Payer Intelligence data as industry insights:
- "The State of Prior Authorization in Dermatology" annual report (use aggregated, anonymized data)
- "Which Payers Are Hardest to Work With?" ranking based on PA approval rates
- "Revenue Left on the Table" ZIP-code analysis tool (public version of Revenue Radar)

Target distribution for content:
- Dermatology Times, Practical Dermatology, Cutis (derm trade publications)
- American Academy of Dermatology (AAD) conference presence
- LinkedIn posts targeting dermatology practice managers and billing specialists
- Local medical society newsletters in NY metro area

**Channel 4: Derm conferences and events**
Not a booth. Booths are expensive and practices don't buy software at booths. Instead:
- Attend AAD Annual Meeting and Innovation Academy
- Host a small breakfast or lunch session for independent practice owners: "How Independent Derm Practices Can Compete with PE-Backed Groups on PA Efficiency"
- Hand out Revenue Radar reports at the session (printed, personalized per attendee's practice ZIP code)
- Follow up within 48 hours

**Channel 5: Second PM integration**
Adding Nextech opens up a second large pool of derm practices. Many practices not on ModMed are on Nextech. This roughly doubles your addressable market without changing anything about the product or sales motion.

**Key metric**: 30-50 practices by month 18. $250K-450K ARR.

### Phase 4: Scale and Multi-Specialty (Months 18-24+)

**Goal**: Expand beyond dermatology. Position for acquisition.

**Next specialties (in order of PA burden)**:
1. Rheumatology (heavy biologic PA, same payers, same documentation requirements)
2. Gastroenterology (biologics for IBD, similar PA patterns)
3. Neurology (MS treatments require extensive PA)
4. Oncology (growing PA requirements for targeted therapies)

Each specialty expansion follows the same playbook:
- Find a design partner in the new specialty
- Adapt the payer rules engine for specialty-specific procedures
- Build a Revenue Radar dataset for that specialty
- Leverage existing payer intelligence data (payer behavior applies across specialties)

**Acquisition positioning**:
By this point, MedEdge should have:
- 50-150+ practices across 2-3 specialties
- $500K-2M ARR
- A proprietary Payer Intelligence Network with 5,000+ PA outcomes
- Revenue Radar data showing market opportunity across multiple specialties
- Auto-Appeal Engine with documented revenue recovery rates
- ModMed + Nextech integrations (+ potentially NextGen)

This is an attractive acquisition target for: ModMed (adds PA to their platform), clearinghouses (completes the pre-claim workflow), RCM companies (bolts onto existing services), or PE practice platforms (operational efficiency at scale).

## Sales Process (Phase 2-3)

### Lead qualification
- Are they a small/mid derm practice (1-10 providers)?
- Do they use ModMed PM?
- Do they accept payers that require PA? (If they avoid all PA-heavy payers, the pain is there but they may not see the value until you show the Revenue Radar)
- Are they independent (not PE-backed)? PE practices have centralized procurement.

### Sales cycle
Target: 2-4 weeks from first conversation to signed contract. Healthcare sales cycles are typically 6-12 months, but small practices decide fast because the decision maker (the doctor/owner) is the same person who feels the PA pain every day. Our advantage is that we sell to the person who has the problem, not a procurement committee.

### Pricing objection handling
"It's cheaper than hiring a person." A dedicated PA staff member costs $40K-55K/year. MedEdge at $299/provider for a 3-provider practice costs $10,764/year. That's 20-25% of the cost of a human, and it works 24/7, never calls in sick, and never quits.

"Show me the ROI." Revenue Radar shows the revenue opportunity. Auto-appeal data shows recovery from denials. Combined, the ROI story is: "MedEdge costs you $897/month. It enables you to accept payers worth $3,000-8,000/month in new revenue, and it recovers $1,000-2,000/month in denied claims. Net ROI: 4-10x your subscription."

### Contract structure
- Month-to-month. No annual commitments required. If the product delivers value, they stay. If it doesn't, they leave and we learn why.
- First 30 days free for practices referred through dad's network (reduces friction).
- Billing through Stripe. Auto-renewal. Simple.
